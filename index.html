All About Atoms

Modern day atom model (including quantum mechanics and quarks)

Atoms are the essential building block for matter, similar to cells are the building block for life. One cannot break up these atoms unless using an atom smasher, i.e the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Elements of the periodic table are pure substances, meaning that they are not made up of an another element. The radius of an atom is extremely small, it is well under 10^-9, which is under a billionth of a meter which is a nanometer. The size varies depending on the type of the nucleus. In the centre of the atom is the nucleus, which consists of protons and neutrons. The prefixes of neutron and proton show it's charge, neutral and positive respectively. Surrounding the nucleus is electrons, which have a negative charge. In a normal isotope of helium has two electrons (negatively charged), two protons (positively charged), and two neutrons (neutral). As a result, the positives and negatives are "canceled out", therefore meaning that the atom is neutral.


Also included are quarks and leptons. Quarks make up neutrons and protons. These quarks are held together via gluons and pions which are the strong force. Quarks come in 6 different types (excluding antiparticles), which in include, Up, Down, Charm, Strange, Top and Bottom. Up and Down are stable quarks while the rest aren't. Up, Charm, Top are all positively charged +2/3, while Down, Strange, Bottom are negatively charged -1/3. Hadrons are particles made out of quarks. A typical proton would have two Ups and one Down, therefore making positive charge, since 2(+2/3) + (-1/3) = 3/3 = 1. Neutrons have two Downs, and one Up, therefore making it 2(-1/3) + 2/3 = 0. Neutrons and Protons are also called baryons, which react with the four fundamental forces that govern the universe, strong force, weak force, electromagnetic force, and gravity.

Subatomic particles are divided in to Bosons and Fermions, based on the quantum spin. Boson have an integer spin, 0 or 1. Different types of gauge bosons include gluons (strong force), photon (electromagnetic force), Z and W boson carry the weak force. Excluded is the Higgs Boson, which gives subatomic particles it's mass via the Higgs field. Fermions have half integer spins, which are divided in to quarks and leptons. As aforementioned above, quarks have 6 types (excluding it's antiparticle counterpart). These quarks combine to make a baryon, which react to the four fundamental forces aforementioned above. Leptons have six types, the electron, muon and tau, which are charged, and three other types: electron neutrino, muon neutrino, and tau neutrino which are neutral in charge. Neutrinos are very small making it almost undetectable.


Democritus' idea of an atom

Democritus was an early greek philosopher. Back in those times, the Greeks debated on what was right, and what seemed right. Back then, they didn't have experiments, it was basically whoever has the better argument was the ideology of the greeks. Aristotle, who was a very influential person, said that a sand particle can be divided infinitesimally. However, Democritus thought that there must be a limit to this "splitting of sand grains", and thought of the atom. The word "atom" comes from greek, meaning "uncuttable" or "indivisible". Atomists at that time believe that there was reality and the "void". Democritus also believe that there were infinitely many atoms but there are different types of them, with different types of shapes and sizes. The "void" was empty space, where atoms collided, connected or repel from each other. Democritus believe that these atoms had "hooks" and "barbs" to connect with other atoms. Aristotle disagreed with Democritus, he thought that matter's composition was earth, air, fire and water. Democritus' theory explained things better, but wasn't as influential was Aristotle, therefore Aristotle's ideas prevailed.


Dalton's idea of an atom


After Democritus' idea of an atom was rubbished and kept away for two thousand years, the atom was reintroduced with Dalton. Around the 1800s, John Dalton rediscovered the atom using a series of experiments, unlike what Democritus did which was simply guess work. Dalton used evidence to support his atomic model theory. He studied various kinds of gases observed their properties and hypothesised that their gases must have tiny particles which move around. In addition to that Dalton studied compounds which consists of two or more elements, and proved that a compound is made out with the same elements with the same whole-number ratio (i.e H2O is 2:1 since there are two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom), and different compounds are have different elements with different ratios. Therefore, Dalton assumed that compounds are made out of atoms which cannot be subdivided. Dalton's atomic theory states that:


1) all matter are made out of atoms which are indivisible and indestructible


2) all atoms of an element are exactly the same


3) compounds are formed via two or more different elements


4) chemical reactions are simply the rearrangement of the atoms.


Compared to the modern era's model, this is almost correct. One of the facts it missed out are various kinds of isotopes, which have differing types of atoms (i.e mass), within an element. These isotopes have the same chemical properties. We also know that we can destroy atoms via nuclear reactions. This model is also missing electrons, protons, and neutrons with their charged properties.


Thomson's idea of an atom


Thomson incorporated the idea of electrons towards Dalton's model. Electricity is simply a stream of negatively charged electrons. Thomson also discovered that an atom can be broken up even further, leading with protons, neutrons and electrons. He got a nobel prize on how gases conduct electricity. This research led to the electron. He discovered the electron via running a electrical current through a cathode ray tube. Inside the "tube" is almost a vacuum, which has an electrode on the opposite sides. These electrodes are electrically conductive, therefore they are made out a metal. There are two types of electrodes, a cathode and an anode. Cathodes are negatively charged, while anodes are positively charged. When the high voltage power supply is turned on, the cathode ray travels toward the cathode. The anodes electrons are being "pumped" towards the cathode, thereby leaving a positive charge. This cathode ray completed the circuit, but Thomson wanted to know what this "cathode ray" was. Thomson then placed one negatively charged and one positively charged plates next to the anode. This cathode ray was replied from the negatively charged plate and attracted to positively charged plate. Therefore, Thomson concluded that there must be something called an "electron". He then measured the mass of the electron by varying the voltage and measuring how much the cathode ray bend. Since these electrons are negative, and atoms are neutral, he hypothesised that an atom was positively charged, making the plum pudding model. The plums were electrons and the pudding was neutral.


Rutherford's idea of an atom

Succeeding Thomson's plum pudding model was Rutherford's ideas about the nucleus. Rutherford discovered that atoms emit positive alpha particles (which is now known as helium nuclei). Rutherford was curious, and wanting to find the structure and purposes of the "alpha particles". To find out, Rutherford aimed a beam of positive alpha particles to a sheet of gold that is a mere 0.0004 cm thick, with a positive charged screen that emits an indicator detecting the positive charged particles. After looking at the evidence, Rutherford was surprised, since the alpha particles went through the god sheet. This meant that atoms were mostly made out of empty space. In addition to that, since the alpha particles are positive and most still went through, that means that the nucleus was neutral. Rutherford inferred that the nucleus contained some positive charged particles, which Rutherford named the proton.
